Art Auction App


Roles

Lead UX Designer , Researcher


Target Audience

An app for busy gallery managers which streamlines the art auction process.


Constraints & Challenges

The main challenge was including a full range of gallery managment functions in a simplified app.


Research Study Details

User research revealed 2 main pain points in running an art auction:

  1. Complicated:

    Most auction software has a steep learning curve, requiring extra training for new hires. Having an intuitive design is key to saving users time and energy on onboarding.

  2. Repetitive:

    Having multiple platforms for inventory, auctions and reporting leads to repetitive data entry at each stage of a pieces’ life in the gallery. Having inventory management, auction, and reporting features built into the app reduce redundant data entry.

user testing results

We performed two rounds of remote, supervised usability testing, which garnered the following findings:


Round 1 findings

  1. Users wanted the freedom to select a date or time independent of each other in the scheduling process.

  2. Users wanted to see what the “view pieces” and plus buttons did on the add pieces screen.

Round 2 findings

  1. Users thought that the local navigation buttons at the top of the screens were too large.

High-Fidelity Designs

Our usability studies provided us with the design direction needed to bring the design into a final high-fidelity design.


Conclusion & Next Steps
